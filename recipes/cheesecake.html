Cheesecake

Description

Cheesecake is always an ideal dessert to serve at a dinner party. The great thing about it is that you can prepare it a day in advance, allowing it to set overnight. The next day, all you need to do is slice and serve it. In this recipe, I’ll explain how to make a delicious cheesecake with a soft, creamy filling combined with a crunchy biscuit base. This remains my favorite cheesecake recipe because you don’t need much baking experience to make it, and it’s easy to vary with different flavors and toppings. Steps

  • Preheat the oven to 140°C using the fan (convection) setting. Place a baking dish filled with water at the bottom of the oven. This helps keep the air moist and reduces the chance of cracks forming in the cheesecake.
  • Cut a circle of baking paper to fit the base of the springform pan. Grease the pan and place the baking paper on the bottom.
  • Place the biscuits in a food processor and blend until fine crumbs form. Melt the butter in a small saucepan and mix it with the crushed biscuits. Spread the mixture evenly over the base of the springform pan and press it down firmly with the back of a spoon to create a solid crust.
  • Add the cream cheese, sugar, vanilla sugar, and eggs to a bowl and mix until combined. Add the crème fraîche and cornstarch and briefly mix again. Pour the cream cheese mixture into the springform pan over the biscuit base.
  • Bake for approximately 50 minutes. Then turn off the oven and allow the cheesecake to cool inside the oven with the door slightly open. Once it has reached room temperature, place it in the refrigerator and let it set for at least 6 hours (or overnight).

Ingredients

  1. 175 g Bastogne biscuits
  2. 50 g butter or margarine
  3. 600 g cream cheese (at room temperature) (Philadelphia)
  4. 100 g sugar
  5. 1 sachet vanilla sugar
  6. 3 eggs (at room temperature)
  7. 200 ml crème fraîche (at room temperature)
  8. 1 tablespoon cornstarch
